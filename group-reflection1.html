Group Reflection

Thanh Long Nguyen

We have been worked as a team for more than three months now, what I can say is the group worked really well together on completing this assignment 3. As mentioned, we used Instagram to communicate since the beginning, and I would say it is not the most effective, but it still gets the works done. One surprising thing is that everyone was hugely responsible for their assigned jobs, each person pulled their weight and fairly contribute to the assignment. Somewhat, communication is the most important thing when it comes to group work, from what we have learned from the feedback of the previous assignment, we now implemented a new way to keep up with relatively amount of Github log of activity. From my perspective, the group worked extremely well and we finished the assignment on time without any conflicts or arguments. However, everything has a space of improvement, although this might be the last project I work with these friendly and hard-working people, I would want us to meet face-to-face more often to get to know each other and improve trust which then increases productivity and efficiency rather than just communicate through Internet.

Workload was distributed through a random generator with two task to complete for everyone, if any task were hard to operate, group members bonded together in the completion of the task. Chloe Henry

There were no issues in terms of how the group worked during this assignment, much like the previous assignment. All team members worked hard in order to get their sections of the project done, with individuals offering to help each other complete sections they believed they would be able to help with. There was a lot more group collaboration this time around when completing the assignment I believe, which helped to create an application that everyone was passionate about and could discuss if asked questions about. Whilst last assignment we used our instagram chat to ask each other questions, this time around we had much larger discussion regarding the work and how we were going to complete it. Like previously mentioned by the other members, there were some weeks where team members could not attend tutorials due to illness, which made it a little more difficult to have group discussions. However, this did not greatly affect our work, as each member completed their share of the work to a high standard. I have nothing negative to say about this group, as everyone pulled their weight and was understanding of each other if there was some difficulty with completing work or attending classes. In terms of improvement, if this group was to ever to work together again, I hope that we could meet more often in person to collaborate on certain parts of the project where collaboration is needed, in order to get a more universal and concise understanding of our application’s purpose, functions and ultimate goal. As a group, we communicate more through google docs than the GitHub repository, hence why there are not many comments. The repository does not reflect the overall collaboration of the group, nor how effectively we completed our work; as our google documents and changes history are a much more effective demonstration of our team work, and the distribution of work between members.

James Matthew Bird

Overall the group worked smoothly and there was little to none problems throughout the whole assignment period. We really expanded our ideas from assignment 2 and have put a lot of time and effort into making sure we covered everything that is required. We had days where people were sick and couldn’t come to class but we still managed fine and everyone completed their work.
